# hanme 🎶

*The accumulated grief of a people — wound carried across generations of unacknowledged injustice that, voiced and witnessed, becomes the ground of their most beautiful making.*

hanme welds two roots from two language isolates with no shared ancestor: Korean **han** (한, 恨), the accumulated grief, resentment of unacknowledged injustice, and longing for what is irrecoverably lost that solidifies across generations into a social-historical wound; and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. So hanme is *han* held as a cosmic ordinance — not individual psychology and not a pathology to be cured, but a received condition of the world: that accumulated, unacknowledged suffering, when fully voiced, generates creative force. It is the cathedral's first Koreanic entry and its first double-isolate compound — the maximum family-diversity configuration available.

hanme names what no English word names and what no other cataloged tradition has articulated this precisely: the wound that *is* the instrument. It is pansori sung from a voice that earned its *han*; it is the blues that is the Middle Passage transformed into beauty; it is the *gut* ceremony that begins in grief and, through *pulda* (the untying), arrives at *sinmyeong* (collective divine-human joy). Its light is that the cosmos does not waste properly-voiced suffering; its shadow is the wound hoarded instead of sounded, or borrowed by those who never earned it. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **7.85/10**.
